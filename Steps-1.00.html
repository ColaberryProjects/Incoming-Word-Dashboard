PROBLEM STATEMENT 
Research shows that highly developed numeracy and literacy capabilities strongly contribute to the social, economic, and physical wellbeing of individuals. The building blocks for literacy start early in life and are a predictor of later literacy, academic achievement, and in general limited opportunities in life. Factors affecting successful outcomes include home environment (including whether books are available at home and whether parents read aloud to their children), availability of resources such as libraries,  level of education of parents and their socioeconomic status. For example, only 57% of Australian Indigenous students, who did not have these opportunities,  reached Intermediate international benchmark, as compared to 83 % of non-Indigenous students.
